Baby Exercises & Activities

Baby & Exercises


Babies and toddlers also need sufficient physical activity, similar to older children. As infants mature, parents can introduce helpful exercises and teach them in appropriate ways. Engaging in physical activity contributes to a baby's comprehensive growth and development. Moreover, it establishes a foundation for children to embrace an active lifestyle early on. This article explores the significance of exercises for infants and presents a range of exercises tailored to different age brackets.


Why should babies exercise? ▼

Doing exercises helps babies learn how to move their bodies and discover new things around them. It also brings these good things:

  • - Makes bones, muscles, and joints stronger.

  • - Helps the brain grow by learning new skills.

  • - Makes it easier to balance and move around.

  • - Helps babies sleep better.

  • - Keeps their body organs healthy.

  • - Makes them feel confident and better at playing with others.

  • - Helps babies stay a good size and weight.

And even if they don't exercise, doing things like reading stories, singing, or drawing can also help babies learn and think.


Exercise tips for babies ▼

Here are some suggestions to consider when it comes to exercising with your baby:

  • - Make sure your baby has playtime on the floor while being supervised, starting from birth to their first year.

  • - Aim for a bit of physical activity each day, and allow them time to crawl around.

  • - If your baby isn't crawling yet, encourage them to hold, push, pull, and move their head, hands, and feet.

  • - Give your baby about 30 minutes of tummy time daily when they're awake.

  • - Once your baby starts crawling, give them a safe space where they can practice.

  • - Let your baby hold onto stable objects and try standing up.

  • - During tummy time, put toys just out of reach to encourage reaching.

  • - Find a balance between indoor and outdoor play to help their senses develop.

  • - Avoid keeping your baby restrained or inactive for more than an hour.

  • - Stay engaged with your baby to help them explore their surroundings.

  • - Use a rug or blanket at least five to seven feet long for their play area to keep them safe.






Baby Activities

Here is a breakdown of the month-by-month activities done by a baby:


Activities of a Newborn ▼

Right after being born, your newborn will spend most of their time sleeping and wake up mainly for feedings. This leaves little time for play. Their eyesight is blurry and can only see about 8 to 10 inches, roughly the distance between your arms and their face. They will develop the ability to focus on objects and coordinate their hand-eye movements in the next two to three months.

Despite having limited skills, your baby is already interested in learning from you. Research indicates that newborns are most captivated by looking at faces, especially yours. Even though they can only communicate through crying at this point, they enjoy hearing you talk and sing. Physical touch, like skin-to-skin contact and baby massages, is beneficial for both you and your baby, fostering bonding and relaxation.


Activities of a 1-Month-Old ▼

Congratulations on completing the first month! By the end of this month or the start of the next, you might witness a big achievement: your baby's first genuine smile aimed at you! This smile is a signal that your baby is becoming ready to interact with the world around them.

Since your baby hasn't quite figured out how to use their hands yet, they'll depend on you to help them explore their sense of touch. Allowing them to touch objects with different textures and temperatures can aid their exploration.

This month can be demanding due to lack of sleep. When you're feeling up to it and have the energy, here are a few straightforward activities you can attempt with your 1-month-old baby. Look for signs that they're prepared to engage, like being calm and attentive. And remember, if they look away, close their eyes, arch their backs, or cry, it's a sign they might need a break.


Activities of a 2-Month-Old ▼

At some point during this month, your baby will begin to produce their first vowel sounds, such as "oh-oh." They'll coo, make gurgling sounds, and sigh in your direction. The effort you put into tummy time will start showing results as your baby can now briefly lift their head while on their tummy and turn it from side to side. Try to have several short tummy time sessions each day.

Activities that involve touch, movement, music, and talking tend to be enjoyed by babies at the 2-month mark. Simply finding opportunities to talk to and hold your baby can assist them in learning about the world around them and their role within it.


Activities of a 3-Month-Old ▼

At 3 months old, your baby is probably captivated by your face. They'll also show a newfound interest in colorful shapes and patterns. If you place an intriguing toy within their sight, they might try reaching for it and swatting at it.

Your baby will keep developing their language skills by listening to your conversations and experimenting with their own sounds. Music remains a wonderful and interactive method to bond with your baby and introduce them to language.


Activities of a 4-Month-Old ▼

Around 4 months of age, your baby's motor skills will noticeably improve. At this stage, they'll have better control over their hands and will start reaching for objects and bringing them to their mouth. Placing toys within their reach will likely grab their attention, keeping them engaged and learning. Let them explore and put objects in their mouth, as this is how they learn. Just be cautious to avoid anything that could be a choking hazard.

During tummy time, position intriguing toys within their line of sight and reach. Regular tummy time sessions will help strengthen their neck and back muscles, setting the stage for rolling over and sitting up.

You might also notice your baby laughing, which adds an extra element of joy to your playtime and activities!


Activities of a 5-Month-Old ▼

At this point, your baby might be able to sit up with a little help. Towards the end of this month, they could achieve a major milestone and start rolling over both from tummy to back and back to tummy.

Around the age of 5 months, your baby could develop the ability to hold onto small objects and might enjoy banging them on the floor or other solid surfaces.

Engaging in activities that promote movement exploration and help them become more familiar with their bodies will keep your baby entertained and intrigued.


Activities of a 6-Month-Old ▼

You've probably noticed that your 6-month-old is quite enamored with their own reflection in the mirror. While it will take some time before they recognize themselves (around 18 months or so), they're captivated by faces and might even smile at their own image. Activities involving mirrors and face interactions are a big hit for 6-month-olds, providing another chance to communicate and bond with your baby.

As their hand control improves, babies at this stage are eager to grasp and manipulate objects, experimenting with turns and twists. Engaging in activities that boost hand-eye coordination will keep your baby curious, occupied, and learning how to interact with their environment.


Activities of a 7-Month-Old ▼

Babies at this age find joy in their own babbles. Even though it may sound like random sounds, your baby is actually starting to imitate the patterns of language they hear from you. Baby babble has recognizable rhythms that set the stage for them to say their first words. Engaging your baby in conversations and talking to them nurtures this essential skill.

You can try narrating your day-to-day activities to your baby. Asking questions and then waiting for their gurgles, babbles, or little kicks in response is a way to create meaningful interactions. By doing this, you're having meaningful conversations and laying the groundwork for their developing brain!

At this point, your baby might also be exploring movements that are a precursor to crawling, like pushing up on their hands and knees and rocking back and forth. Activities that promote these motions will help them build the strength and coordination necessary for independent movement – whether that's crawling, scooting, or some other form.


Activities of an 8-Month-Old ▼

At 8 months old, a whole new world of understanding opens up for your baby. They'll start grasping the concept of cause and effect. It's delightful for them when their favorite toy's beads create a rattling sound or when splashes happen in the bath each time they kick their legs.

Around this age, your baby will also begin comprehending "object permanence." In simple words, this means they realize that things still exist even if they can't see them anymore. This is why games like peek-a-boo become so enjoyable for them. Engaging in activities that highlight this exciting realization will truly capture your little one's interest.


Activities of a 9-Month-Old▼

As your 9-month-old becomes more coordinated, they'll naturally want to explore their own body and its abilities. You might notice them touching their toes and even trying to put them in their mouth. They could also push their feet off the ground, bouncing a bit when you hold them upright – a small step towards walking. Engaging in activities that introduce and name body parts can be both fun and educational.

Furthermore, continue nurturing their verbal skills by reading and talking to them regularly. Encourage their inner scientist by letting them drop, tap, poke, fill, and squeeze objects, letting them observe the effects they can create through their actions. This is a wonderful way to help them learn about cause and effect in an interactive manner.


Activities of a 10-Month-Old ▼

Exciting milestones are on the horizon as your baby gears up for the skills needed to walk. This includes pulling up to stand and eventually "cruising" while holding onto furniture. Make sure the environment is safe and enjoyable, then watch your baby explore!

Another crucial skill your baby is refining now is hand-eye coordination. Around 10 months, they'll likely be intrigued by objects in and out of containers and even trying to feed themselves (or play with their food). This is a great time to introduce a variety of flavors and textures, so don't hesitate to serve up those veggies and fruits and let them dive in!


Activities of an 11-Month-Old ▼

By now, you might have noticed that your 11-month-old baby is responding to simple requests like "wave bye-bye!" They might even be using simple words like "mama" and "dada." Music is a wonderful tool to support these budding language abilities. You can make it even more fun by adding some "dancing" to the rhythm, which also helps improve coordination and gross motor skills.

Speaking of motor skills, your baby is also getting better at fine motor skills. They're using their pincer grasp to pick up small objects using their thumb and fingers. Engaging in activities that involve manipulating objects, sifting, measuring, and pouring can greatly contribute to the development of these important hand skills.


Activities of a 1-Year-Old ▼

Congratulations on reaching this exciting milestone – your baby's first year! This marks a remarkable period as babies are either close to reaching or have already achieved a wide range of milestones. From moving beyond babbling to saying their initial words, to taking their first steps in a few months, there's so much growth ahead.

Around the age of 1 year, babies hit another language milestone: understanding words. They begin responding to simple phrases like "no" or "where's the doggy?" and recognizing their own name. Soon, your child might start imitating you, like pretending a block is a "phone." Using gestures like pointing, waving, clapping, and blowing a kiss is a significant part of language development, so encourage these actions.

Provide your budding toddler with space to explore, and ensure there are plenty of toys within their reach. This will support their cognitive, language, social, and motor development in these crucial early stages.






0 - 3 Months Baby Game & Activity Recommendations

Let's check them out!


Week 1 Activities ▼

  • Tummy to Play:
    Begin incorporating short sessions of tummy time into your baby's routine to help them develop core strength.

  • Face-to-Face:
    Spend time lying down with your baby on your chest, encouraging them to strengthen their core muscles and achieve developmental milestones.


Week 2 Activities ▼

  • Evolving Mobile:
    Keep your baby engaged by changing the toys on their mobile or play mat every couple of weeks, stimulating their vision.

  • Sleeping Direction:
    Change the direction your baby lies while sleeping to help them build strength by turning in different directions.


Week 3 Activities ▼

  • Beep Baby:
    Engage your baby's sense of touch and body awareness by tapping them in different places while naming each body part.

  • Mirror on the Wall:
    Introduce your baby to their reflection in a child-safe mirror, aiding their visual development.


Week 4 Activities ▼

  • Head Lifts:
    Encourage your baby's head lifting during tummy time by dangling a toy for them to look at.

  • Massage Feeding:
    Give your baby a gentle massage on their arms, legs, and back before feeding to increase their alertness for feeding.


Week 5 Activities ▼

  • Smiling Faces:
    Show your baby pictures of smiling faces to help them focus and develop their ability to recognize faces.

  • Soft Touch:
    Provide your baby with a soft toy to feel and interact with, promoting their sense of touch and visual focus.


Week 6 Activities ▼

  • Mirrors All Around:
    Take your baby around the house to see their reflection in mirrors, helping them connect with their own image.

  • Songs in Motion:
    Sing songs with hand motions to captivate your baby's attention and enhance their language skills.


Week 7 Activities ▼

  • Tap, Tap, Tap:
    Tap your baby's feet in rhythm with a song to introduce them to new sounds and improve their listening skills.

  • Hand Claps:
    Gently clap your baby's hands to music, helping them become aware of their body and rhythm.


Week 8 Activities ▼

  • Gentle Dance:
    Dance with your baby to music, helping them get accustomed to new sounds and words.

  • Reach For It:
    Encourage your baby to touch and interact with toys, enhancing their hand movement coordination.


Week 9 Activities ▼

  • Cooing & Gurgling:
    Engage your baby in conversations by talking to them and imitating their cooing and gurgling sounds.

  • Switch Swat:
    Dangle objects for your baby to swipe at, improving their hand-eye coordination.


Week 10 Activities ▼

  • Parent Talkathon:
    Talk to your baby using a slow, melodic voice to capture their attention and encourage them to copy your mouth movements.

  • Cheesy Smile:
    Share big smiles and funny noises with your baby, promoting bonding and interaction.


Week 11 Activities ▼

  • Play Peek-a-Boo:
    Play peek-a-boo while your baby is lying on their tummy, promoting play and memory skills.

  • Tongue Teezer:
    Encourage your baby to stick out their tongue during playtime or diaper changes, supporting tongue control.


Week 12 Activities ▼

  • Side Chats:
    Lie baby down and talk to them from different sides to encourage them to turn their head.

  • Express Yourself:
    Use a variety of facial expressions while interacting with your baby to help them learn about emotions.


Week 13 Activities ▼

  • Busy Fingers:
    Allow your baby to play with their fingers and hands, exploring their surroundings and improving hand-eye coordination.

  • Sense of Touch:
    Gently touch your baby's feet and tummy to observe their reactions and help them develop sensory skills.






4 - 6 Months Baby Game & Activity Recommendations

Let's check them out!


Week 14 Activities ▼

  • Blow Raspberries:
    Before dressing baby, blow air on their tummy to create a funny noise. This encourages baby to make their own noises and lip movements, which aids in babbling and copying sounds. Helps baby build communication skills.

  • Put on a Show!:
    Perform something silly in front of baby, like dancing or acting out animal movements. This helps baby learn to focus their attention and follow moving objects.


Week 15 Activities ▼

  • Copy-Cat Chit-Chat:
    Respond to baby's coos and babbles as if having a conversation. This helps baby learn the give and take of communication and encourages them to babble and imitate sounds.

  • Roll Baby Roll:
    Place baby on their side during Tummy Time and encourage them to roll onto their tummy. Helps baby strengthen core muscles for rolling.


Week 16 Activities ▼

  • Toy Challenge #1:
    Hold baby and encourage them to reach for a toy, switching it from one hand to the other. This helps baby work on hand-eye coordination.

  • Elevator Fun:
    Pretend baby is riding an elevator by gently lifting them up and down while lying on your back. Helps baby experience different types of movement.


Week 17 Activities ▼

  • Action Songs:
    Sing action rhymes like "This Little Piggy" while interacting with baby's fingers and toes. Helps baby develop body awareness.

  • A Song to Move the Job Along:
    Sing a song about actions as you perform them, like opening and closing doors. This helps baby learn words that describe actions and objects.


Week 18 Activities ▼

  • On and Off:
    Show baby how to turn things on and off, like lights or water faucets, using simple words like "on" and "off." Helps baby understand cause and effect.

  • Tummy Time Peek-a-Boo:
    Play peek-a-boo while baby is on their tummy. This makes Tummy Time fun and helps baby improve core strength.


Week 19 Activities ▼

  • Lifting Up and Down:
    Gently lift baby above your head and bring them back down. This helps baby develop their sense of balance.

  • Light Moves:
    Gently move your knees up and down and side to side while baby sits on your lap. This helps baby experience new types of movement.


Week 20 Activities ▼

  • Body Massage:
    Give baby a gentle massage after bath or during changing. This helps baby bond with you and use their senses of touch and vision.

  • Noise Makers:
    Give baby toys that make noise, like rattles, and encourage them to explore different ways of playing with them. Helps baby learn about cause and effect.


Week 21 Activities ▼

  • Sightseeing with Baby:
    Use a carrier or wrap while you do household chores. This helps baby develop balance and explore their surroundings.

  • Roll Me!:
    Help baby roll over by guiding their motion with one leg. This helps baby learn to roll.


Week 22 Activities ▼

  • A Solid Move:
    Introduce smooth purees after feeding baby milk. This helps baby transition to solid foods and develop feeding skills.

  • Messy Eater:
    Allow baby to touch and play with their food to explore different textures. This helps baby learn about food textures.


Week 23 Activities ▼

  • Taking the Spotlight:
    Engage with baby and encourage them to communicate by making sounds and using gestures. This helps baby learn to communicate and engage with caregivers.

  • Roll Away:
    Encourage baby to roll over by placing toys just out of their reach. This helps baby develop core muscle strength.


Week 24 Activities ▼

  • Break Out the Board Books:
    Read books to baby with colorful pictures and describe the images. This helps baby develop vision and listening skills.

  • Nature Walks:
    Take baby for a walk outside to expose them to new sounds and sensations. This helps baby experience the world around them.


Week 25 Activities ▼

  • Chatter Box:
    Talk to baby often, describing your actions and listening to their responses. This helps baby develop their language skills and communication.

  • Pureed Food:
    Introduce new pureed foods to baby and wait before introducing another food to check for allergies. This helps baby develop feeding and swallowing skills.


Week 26 Activities ▼

  • Floor Explorer:
    Create a safe space for baby to explore on the floor and engage with them using toys and interactions. This helps baby improve their neck and head control and strengthens muscles.

  • Lean and Reach:
    Place toys just out of baby's reach to encourage them to shift their weight and reach for the toys. This helps baby develop motor skills.






7 - 9 Months Baby Game & Activity Recommendations

Let's check them out!


Week 27 Activities ▼

  • Sign Language:
    Introduce basic sign language gestures alongside words to help baby communicate. This includes signs for "more" and "all done." This aids in the development of early communication skills.

  • Board Book Fun:
    Choose interactive board books with elements like fold-outs and noises. Allow baby to explore the book at their own pace, promoting language skills and sensory engagement.


Week 28 Activities ▼

  • Moo and Baa:
    Incorporate animal sounds while playing or reading to baby. Associate pictures of animals with their respective sounds to enhance auditory recognition.

  • Name Game:
    Point to facial features and name them for baby. Observe baby's reactions as you engage them in naming different parts of your face.


Week 29 Activities ▼

  • Shadow-Puppet Play:
    Use your hands to create shadow puppets on a surface while talking and singing. This encourages visual tracking skills in baby.

  • Be Like Baby:
    Emulate baby's actions and movements. When baby performs an action, repeat it back to them. This enhances joint attention and social interaction.


Week 30 Activities ▼

  • Wet Washcloth:
    Offer a frozen washcloth for baby to bite on to soothe teething discomfort.

  • Roll and Pull:
    Use a piece of yarn with an object tied to it for baby to pull. Demonstrate how to pull the yarn to bring the object closer. This supports gross motor skill development.


Week 31 Activities ▼

  • Sitting Up to Play:
    Now that baby can sit independently, roll a ball to them and encourage them to roll it back using both hands. This strengthens back, shoulder muscles, and core.

  • Little Explorer:
    Allow baby to explore the environment by touching and grabbing objects within reach, fostering new sensory experiences.


Week 32 Activities ▼

  • Command Excitement:
    Give baby simple commands and express excitement when they follow through. This helps baby learn to listen and respond to directions.

  • Splish Splash:
    Provide a tub of water with floating objects for baby to splash and interact with. Always supervise to ensure safety. This activity enhances sensory and motor skills.


Week 33 Activities ▼

  • Toy Grasp Time:
    When baby holds toys in both hands, introduce a third toy and observe how they figure out how to grasp it without letting go of the others. This supports fine motor and problem-solving skills.

  • Backyard Fun:
    Allow baby to explore the backyard under supervision, exposing them to new sights, smells, and sounds.


Week 34 Activities ▼

  • Monkey See, Monkey Do:
    Use a mirror to make silly faces and sounds, and observe as baby imitates your gestures. This encourages language sound imitation.

  • Raining in the Bath:
    Create a bath toy by punching holes in a container's lid and filling it with water. Let baby experience "rain" as they pour water out. Ensure constant supervision for safety.


Week 35 Activities ▼

  • Toy Stacking:
    Introduce stacking toys like blocks or rings for baby to explore and interact with. Stacking and knocking them down enhances visual and thinking skills.

  • Horsing Around:
    Sit baby on your knees and simulate a horse-riding motion. This introduces baby to new types of movement and environmental exploration.


Week 36 Activities ▼

  • Tray Time:
    Place pureed foods on baby's tray and encourage play with a spoon. This helps baby develop feeding skills.

  • Train to Crawl:
    Create a tunnel with your legs for baby to crawl through, enhancing gross motor skills.


Week 37 Activities ▼

  • Story Time:
    Describe your actions throughout the day to baby, aiding in listening and communication skills.

  • Music Time:
    Provide baby with a musical instrument toy to experiment with different sounds, promoting sensory engagement.


Week 38 Activities ▼

  • Textures and Temps:
    Allow baby to experience different textures and temperatures with toys, promoting sensory exploration.

  • Move and Crawl:
    Help baby into crawling position and encourage movement toward a toy. This supports motor skill development.


Week 39 Activities ▼

  • Pat-A-Cake:
    Engage in the "Pat-A-Cake" rhyme with baby, guiding them in clapping their hands.

  • Rise to Stand:
    Assist baby from a seated position to standing, helping them strengthen leg muscles for future walking.






10 - 12 Months Baby Game & Activity Recommendations

Let's check them out!


Week 40 Activities ▼

  • Family Meals:
    Involve baby in mealtime by having them join you at the table. Share safe bite-sized foods from your plate to introduce them to different smells and sights of foods.

  • First Time Foods:
    Introduce new foods to baby while they're hungry and more receptive to trying new tastes and textures.


Week 41 Activities ▼

  • Wave Hello:
    Assist baby in waving hello to new people, helping them ease any stranger anxiety.

  • The Daily Read:
    Read books with pictures daily, describing the images to promote language development.


Week 42 Activities ▼

  • Ball Chase:
    Encourage crawling by rolling a ball across the room, prompting baby to crawl after it and enhancing core strength.

  • Shapes, Sizes, and More:
    Describe textures, shapes, and sizes to help baby associate words with objects and experiences.


Week 43 Activities ▼

  • Inside Surprise:
    Place a toy or book inside a wrapped cardboard box for baby to open, promoting fine motor development and language skills.

  • Tray Pick Up:
    Offer finger foods on the highchair tray for baby to pick up and eat, fostering fine motor skills.


Week 44 Activities ▼

  • Old MacDonald:
    Sing repetitive songs like "Old MacDonald Had a Farm" to support language development.

  • Head, Shoulders, Knees, and Toes:
    Sing the song while demonstrating actions, helping baby learn body parts.


Week 45 Activities ▼

  • Enjoy the Outdoors:
    Explore the outdoors with baby, introducing them to different textures and smells in the environment.

  • Buttons, Levers, and More:
    Offer toys with interactive buttons and levers, allowing baby to explore and practice fine motor skills.


Week 46 Activities ▼

  • Towel Time:
    Play peek-a-boo with a towel to teach object permanence.

  • Hide and Find:
    Hide objects or yourself during playtime to enhance baby's understanding of object permanence.


Week 47 Activities ▼

  • Round of Applause:
    Applaud baby's achievements and encourage them to clap along.

  • First Interview:
    Engage baby in conversation, recording their responses and playing it back to develop communication skills.


Week 48 Activities ▼

  • Obstacle Crawls:
    Create obstacle courses for baby to crawl over, under, and through, promoting spatial understanding.

  • Water Bottle Fun:
    Offer a sealed water bottle with objects inside for baby to shake and enhance visual skills.


Week 49 Activities ▼

  • Kickin’ Back:
    Dangle a ball within baby's reach and encourage them to kick it while lying on their back or hitting it while on their tummy.

  • Roll to Me:
    Roll a bouncy ball back and forth with baby to support gross motor skills.


Week 50 Activities ▼

  • Toy Chest:
    Show baby where toys are stored, encouraging them to crawl and explore different toys. Also, involve them in tidying up.

  • Tidy Up:
    Prompt baby to pick up scattered toys and hand them to you, promoting leg muscle strength and basic task completion.


Week 51 Activities ▼

  • Tower Time:
    Build towers using stackable blocks and let baby knock them down, helping them refine motor skills.

  • Big Toy Time:
    Place pillows on the floor for baby to crawl over, supporting crawling development and spatial understanding.


Week 52 Activities ▼

  • Squeaky Toy Fun:
    Introduce baby to a squeaky toy, covering it with a blanket and encouraging them to locate and reveal the sound.

  • Bubble Time:
    Blow bubbles for baby to pop, teaching cause and effect and enhancing sensory exploration.




Note: Remember, these activities are meant to encourage your baby's development in a playful and engaging manner. Always prioritize safety and enjoy the bonding experience with your baby.
